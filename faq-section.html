Will this affect my credit score?

Enrolling in a debt relief program may have a temporary impact on your credit score, as you are negotiating with creditors to reduce your debt. However, having large amounts of debt already makes it difficult to get approved for loans or other forms of credit. As your debt is reduced and paid off, your overall financial health improves, which can eventually lead to a better credit score. Many participants find that being debt-free not only relieves stress but also improves their chances of securing loans in the future.

What types of debt are covered by this program?

This program is designed to help with unsecured debts, such as credit card debt, personal loans, and medical bills. However, it does not cover secured debts, like mortgages or auto loans. Our team can help you determine whether your specific debt qualifies for the program.

Do I need to be up to date on my payments?

In most cases, yes, you need to be current on your credit card or loan payments to qualify for the program. Being up-to-date on payments shows creditors that you are committed to managing your debt, which can make negotiations more favorable. However, if you've fallen behind, we can still discuss options that may be available to help you get back on track.

How much debt do I need to enroll in the program?

The program requires you to have at least $15,000 in unsecured debt (such as credit card debt, medical bills, or personal loans) to qualify. In fact, the more debt you have, the more the program can save you. Larger debts often mean greater opportunities for negotiation, which can lead to deeper reductions in both your monthly payments and the total amount owed. If you have less than $15,000 in unsecured debt, there are certain circumstances where you can still enroll so we recommend still giving a phone call to see if they can help you.

Will I be able to use my credit cards?

No, your enrolled cards will be rendered inactive. We do recommend, however, that you set aside one card to be used for any emergency situations that might arise. This card ideally should have an available balance.
